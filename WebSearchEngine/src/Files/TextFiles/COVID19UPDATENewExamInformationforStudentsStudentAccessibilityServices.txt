covid-19 update: new exam information for students covid-19 update: new exam information for students covid-19 update: new exam information for students exams will be written either remotely or in-person in the winter 2022 semester based on the mode of course delivery: hybrid courses- if the exam is being written in-person and on-campus, registered sas students can schedule to write their exam in the sas office. if the exam is online and being completed remotely, students do not schedule the exam with sas. face to face courses- if the exam is being written in-person and on-campus, registered sas students can schedule to write their exam in the sas office via aim (snap has been retired). steps on how to do so are available here: https://www.uwindsor.ca/studentaccessibility/sites/uwindsor.ca.studentaccessibility/files/how_to_schedule_an_exam_in_aim_2022.pdf if you do not schedule your exam in accordance with the dates outlined in aim, the steps on how to submit an appeal are available here: https://www.uwindsor.ca/studentaccessibility/sites/uwindsor.ca.studentaccessibility/files/how_to_submit_an_exam_appeal_-_aim.pdf if the exam is online and being completed remotely, students do not schedule the exam with sas. online courses- exams will be written remotely and students do not schedule the exam with sas hyflex courses- exams will be written remotely and students do not schedule the exam with sas a full list of course models is available here. revised assessments have been developed by faculty and will be distributed via new syllabi for each course at the beginning of the fall term. please review these new syllabi to assess what, if any, accommodations will be needed to complete this course work. it remains possible that timed exams may be replaced with written questions or papers. your sas advisor is available through e-mail to respond to questions about accommodations. general: sas@uwindsor.ca exams:
