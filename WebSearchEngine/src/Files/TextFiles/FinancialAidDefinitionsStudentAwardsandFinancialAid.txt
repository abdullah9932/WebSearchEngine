definitions first generation student the following criteria is required for a student to be considered for an award that is designated for a first generation student attending the university of windsor: your parent(s) have never participated in postsecondary studies anywhere in the world on either a full or part-time basis. you?re enrolled in a first-entry undergraduate program. second entry programs (e.g., law or medicine) and graduate programs are not eligible. you don?t already have a postsecondary degree, diploma or certificate. services available: phone you may reach our office at: & updates impact of alternative grading system on osap it is recognized that dropping or withdrawing from course(s) may be necessary to avoid academic standing issues. if you are receiving student financial aid, dropping or withdrawing from course(s) could have both immediate, and future financial implications. prior to dropping or withdrawing from course(s), ensure you understand the impact to your financial aid eligibility and assessment. contact the student awards and financial aid office via the ask.uwindsor.ca platform for further details. impact of alternative grading system on scholarship renewal for renewable scholarship eligibility, students must remain registered in a minimum *60% course load each semester (*covid exception policy for 2020/21 and 2021/22 academic years) and maintain the minimum cumulative average required for their respective awards. if you are considering either the pass/no pass or voluntary withdrawal (vwc) option as outlined in the recent email regarding the alternative grading system, you may wish to submit a service request to our office via the ask.uwindsor.ca platform to discuss the possible impact on your scholarship renewal
