hours new at your library events covid-19 updates connect with your library subscribe to leddy news make a lucky charm button at leddy library this week button-making is back at leddy library! from march 16 - 18, stop by leddy library to make a lucky-charm button. the leddy team will be offering free button-making during regular library hours. make a custom button using your favourite photos and designs or get crafty with the supplies and pre-made templates provided by leddy. the team will have all the equipment and supplies needed from magazines to cut images from, drawing tools, and pre-made templates. if you wish to bring your own pictures, the necessary diameter is 6.5 cm to produce a button 5.6 cm across. the craft table station will be set up in the main building across from the information desk. once designs are complete students can submit their button at the information desk for a staff member to complete. this is our first button-making event since covid limited in-person gatherings. please continue to take health and safety precautions while participating: to enter leddy library all patrons must complete the vaccine attestation and the daily safe lancer self-assessment. masks are to be worn at all times. craft tables are limited to 5 people. please wait in a socially distant line for a space to open before approaching a table. please sanitize your hands before and after using the craft tables. please place used craft supplies in the ?used? container to be sanitized. st. patrick?s day, a religious holiday in ireland, has taken on its own character and spectacle in north america. ?it is a festival of soda bread, corned beef and cabbage, cardboard shamrocks, and green hats, bagels, and beer.? (allen, 2004) this week as st. patrick?s day approaches, we invite you to consider a different side of ireland by exploring some of its influential authors and books. please feel free to explore, borrow and enjoy! essential reading in irish literature collection highlights a sample of influential irish authors and their works. tap into the rich literary history of ireland this saint patrick's day. tags button-making strategic priority exceptional undergraduate experience connect with your library subscribe to leddy news contact us research & reference help library hours my library account wellness blackboard manage uwin account terms and conditions emergency services for students the university of windsor is situated on the traditional territory of the three fires confederacy of first nations: the ojibwa, the odawa, and the potawatomi. follow us on twitter follow us on facebook follow us on linkedin follow us on flickr follow us on youtube follow us on instagram
