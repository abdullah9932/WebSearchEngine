department of co-operative education & workplace partnerships work-integrated learning coordinator ? stem classification "vi" in the department of co-operative education & workplace partnerships employment group list: cupe 1393 posting type: internal job posting number: 2022-13-20 closing date: 11/04/2022 - 4:00pm primary job responsibilities: the work-integrated learning coordinator ? stem will participate in the planning, development and administration of work-integrated learning programs for computer science and stem related programs. the incumbent will coordinate the academic and administrative aspect of the program including intake, employment preparation, and placement of students, job development, student advising and monitoring of work site and facilitation of student reflection. this role involves the promotion and liaison with the academic unit, student body and employer community. essential qualifications: the successful candidate will have: undergraduate degree in computer science or related field as determined by the employer two (2) years full-time work experience in the direct coordination of applied learning programs, human resources, career counseling or employment counselling familiarity with models/theories related to experiential learning, work-integrated learning, and student/career development; familiarity with co-operative education and/or other forms of experiential learning extensive knowledge of employment strategies, the labour market and the local business community in relation to the stem field. intercultural acumen thorough grasp of the rules and regulations of all university of windsor work-integrated learning programs as well as their academic requirements and curriculum (with particular expertise in the designated program e.g. business, computer science, engineering etc..) strong computer skills and competency with excel, word, powerpoint, microsoft outlook, internet and database software packages and familiarity with social media platforms strong communication, organizational, multi-tasking and problem-solving skills ability to work as part of a team willingness to travel occasionally rate of pay: classification "vi" per schedule a of the collective agreement how to apply if you need an accommodation for any part of the application and hiring process, please notify the employment coordinator at extension 4523. should you require further information on accommodations, please visit the office of human rights, equity & accessibility (ohrea). please note that the job postings are removed from this site by the end of business on the closing date listed on the posting. the university thanks all applicants for their interest, however, only those candidates considered for an interview will be contacted.
