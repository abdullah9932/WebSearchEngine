Welcome to FAHSS

Dean’s Welcome Message:

Hello and welcome to the Faculty of Arts, Humanities and Social Sciences at the University of Windsor – or FAHSS, for short! 

The University of Windsor sits on the traditional territory of the Three Fires Confederacy of First Nations, comprised of the Ojibway, the Odawa, and the Potawatomie.  We respect the longstanding relationships with First Nations people in this place in the 100-mile Windsor-Essex peninsula and the straits – les détroits – of Detroit; and are grateful to have the opportunity to teach, research, work and live in this community.

Our Faculty is the largest on campus with approximately 5,000 full and part-time students.  We offer over fifty degree programs and a wide range of unique undergraduate certificates and minors in the fine and performing arts, the humanities and culture, as well as in the social sciences - home to accredited professional programs in Social Work and Psychology.

We pride ourselves in our student-centred approach that allows everyone who comes here the flexibility and support to create their own educational experience whether that be through combining programs and certificates or minors across different areas, taking a double-major degree program, or through immersion in some of our unique interdisciplinary experiences.  These include our Aeronautics Leadership program that offers pilot training alongside a university education; our one-of-a-kind Visual Arts in the Built Environment (VABE) arts-based architecture program; our popular Concurrent Bachelor of Education combined programs offered with our Faculty of Education; alongside value-added certificates and minors in Anthrozoology, Work and Employment, Indigenous Studies, Race & Ethnicity, and Film Studies, to name just a few. I encourage you to peruse our website and drop by for a visit.  We are eager to help you discover your own unique career path and our friendly FAHSS advisors are ready and willing to help you plan your next steps! 

As a member of our FAHSS family, you will not only gain an excellent education, but we also offer important opportunities for you to learn outside of the classroom and to develop life and employment skills including competencies in research and leadership to allow you to help solve the big picture problems of our time.  Undergraduate and graduate research opportunities – in our community and abroad - are readily available in FAHSS, alongside peer mentorship training and application.   We also recognize this training through our LEAD Scholar medallion program upon graduation.

We are here to support you in your journey toward the next chapter in your life.  Our FAHSS Transform program helps students transition from high school to university.  Our FAHSS App allows you easy access to learning communities, peer and mental health supports, important academic and non-academic information, and quick answers to your questions - all at your fingertips through your phone.

Here in FAHSS, your success is our success.  We are excited to be a part of your academic journey, which will undoubtably be “FAHSSome!”   
 

Dr. Cheryl Collier
Dean of FAHSS
Professor, Political Science
