Alumni Cares Volunteer Program

Launched in January 2021, the Alumni Cares volunteer program provides opportunities for alumni and friends of the University of Windsor to give back to their communities in a meaningful and engaging way. Through volunteerism, local and global community outreach and acts of good will, the program seeks to build campus and community partnerships by collaborating to help those in need and supporting the mission of enriching the lives of students and alumni.

Alumni Cares Volunteer Opportunities

Detroit River Watershed Cleanup - April 9th, 10am - 12pm 

The University of Windsor Alumni Association is proud to support the Essex Region Conservation Authority, and the Detroit River Canadian Cleanup in participating to make our local parks a cleaner and safer area for all to enjoy. 

This cleanup will benefit the community by providing educational experiences about litter and waste, fostering environmental stewardship of the Detroit River in participants, cleaning public spaces, improving the aesthetics of the area, and removing trash that could potentially be ingested by pets and wildlife. 

We will be meeting at McKee Park (3026, Sandwich St.) at 10 am. 

 If you are interested in volunteering, click HERE to register! 

Life After 50 Volunteer Opportunities 

The University of Windsor Alumni Association has partnered with Life After 50, offering various volunteer opportunities. 

Telephone Assurance Caller
- Providing a friendly phone call to homebound seniors to ensure safety and general well-being. 
- Calls vary from once a week to 3 times a week, Monday to Friday between 8:30am and 4:30pm

Computer One to One
- Assist members with basic computer skills, cell phone and tablet use. 
- This can be done by appointment, Monday to Friday, 9am to 4pm. 

If you are interested in volunteering, please call or email our Volunteer Coordinator at mhoevenaars@lifeafterfifty.ca, or call 519-254-1108 x132. 

Alumni Snow Angels Volunteers

The University of Windsor Alumni Association has partnered with the City of Windsor's Snow Angels program to help members of the community who need it most during the winter months.

The Snow Angels program has been running for 20 years and many residents rely on the service provided by volunteers. Recently, Snow Angels' volunteer numbers have dropped which means that people like you are needed more than ever. This program has the potential to help over 400 residents of Windsor with their snow removal needs. We thank you for your interest in this program and applaud your willingness to help those in the community who need it most.

Visit the Alumni Snow Angels Volunteer website to learn more and register to volunteer.

Give blood and help save a life. Join the Alumni Association's Partner for Life team with Canadian Blood Services.

The Alumni Association is a Canadian Blood Services’ (CBS) Partner for Life. As Alumni, your donation can count towards our pledge of 50 blood donations in 2021. Friends of the Alumni Association and University of Windsor can also donate to our pledge.

Visit the Alumni Cares Blood Donation website to learn more and book your appointment.

Become a regular Alumni Association Volunteer

The Alumni Association is always seeking positive, energetic individuals who would like to volunteer. The Association has a variety of volunteer opportunities to suit different passions, skill sets and interests.

If you have the time to commit to giving back to the University of Windsor, have received a degree, diploma or certificate in a certified program of one year or more from the University of Windsor or if you're a current student, the Alumni Association has an opportunity for you.

To receive information on all Alumni Association volunteer opportunities, use the button below to complete a volunteer application.

Benefits of Volunteering with the Association

By volunteering with the Alumni Association, individuals reap the following benefits:

  • Make a difference at the University of Windsor and in your community
  • Network and connect with like-minded individuals in the community and meet new people
  • Learn new skills
  • Gain confidence
  • Be a part of the local community
  • Take on a new challenge
  • Have fun
